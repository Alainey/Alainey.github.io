Critique Journals


Overview: As an introduction to UI/UX, I wrote a series of critiques on bad interfaces that I encountered over the course of a week. The four critiques I wrote were posted on Medium, and aim to showcase both my thought process and my writing style. In each publication, I discuss the particularly difficult aspects of each interface, and go on to propose possible reasons as to why the interface may have been designed as it is. I then give my own opinion on how I believe the interface could be improved, and discuss trade offs between my design and the current one.

Read my full critique of Perkins' outlet situation here.

Read my full critique of the Valve Steam Controller here.

Read my full critique of the Instant Pot Duo Mini interface here.

Read my full critique of Goldilocks USA Backshop interface here.

Conclusion: This project was my first true introduction to the world of UI dedsign. At the beginning of the course, I was confused as to why so many students from other diciplines were enrolled - a majority of the class seemed to be non-CS. However, this project helped me understand why. I'd previously approached the field of UI from a graphics/software-only perspective, but design issues are prominant everywhere - not just in digital settings. It was an interesting (if outwardly obvious) relevation for me. In the end, I believe this project was extremely successful in changing my way of thinking and pushing me towards a mindset better greared for quality interface design.
